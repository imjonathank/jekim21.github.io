About Metamorphoses

Apuleius' Metamorphoses (also known as Asinus Aureus) stands out among Latin literature as the sole surviving ancient Roman novel. The story follows a young man named Lucius, who through a series of unfortunate magical events, is turned into a donkey. His efforts to turn himself back into a human lead him across the Roman Empire, encountering a wide variety of people and religions throughout his journey. Most significantly, Lucius undergoes both a physical and spiritual metamorphosis: he is ultimately saved during a festival to the Egyptian goddess Isis, to whom he now pledges cultic devotion. The Metamorphoses, then, is as much an in-depth examination of a young man (a Latin bildungsroman of sorts) as it is an assessment of broader Roman religion.
